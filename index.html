Terminology

Terms can be interpreted in different ways by different people, below our definition of the two key terms in our visualization.

Terrorism

Terrorism is committing or threatening others with violence. Terrorism is directed at people or against society. Usually, the goal is to force political change or to sow fear. When we are talking about a personal attack targeted at one individual, this is not terrorism.

Terrorist attack

These are crimes committed from ideological motives. These are crimes with the aim of social change, to hunt or to influence political decision-making and/or enduce fear onto society. A terrorist attack can consist of a suicide bombing, shootings or other types of attacks.

Our sources

We will use data from the website Global Terrorism Database.

The website Global Terrorism Database was launched by several professors of The University of Maryland. The people responsible for the website are: Gary LaFree and Laura Dugan, Both Professors of Criminology and Criminal Justice at the University of Maryland. Gary LaFree is also the director of START (The National Consortium for the Study of Terrorism and Responses to Terrorism).

The GTD (Global Terrorism Database) gets its data from reports by various media sources. The website promises that no data is added before they are sure that the source is credible. Their information is extracted from more than 4 million news articles and 25,000 news sources. The site is supervised by an advisory committee of 12 experts terrorism investigation.

For more information and a record of all the data: http://www.start.umd.edu/gtd/
